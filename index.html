I currently live and work in Charlotte, North Carolina as a software developer for Signature Consultants, a recruiting and staffing firm in the IT sector. Since August 2020, I've been on assignment with one of their clients in the banking industry. I design, code, test, and debug server-side applications for Linux environments. Unlike many other developers, I have had no formal education in computer science or software engineering; I'm self-taught.

I did my undergraduate and graduate studies at the University of South Carolina. In 2015, I completed my bachelor's degree with a double major in Philosophy and Classics. At that time, I was obsessed with logic, ancient physics, and philology. In 2017, I completed my master's degree in Higher Education. As a graduate student, I was very interested in the social sciences—psychology, sociology, economics, and the law—and I used my electives to take courses in statistics. I also held an assistantship with the university's housing department for the duration of the degree which introduced me to data management and relational databases.

Instead of entering higher education administration after graduate school like most of my cohort, I leveraged the skills I had gained in data analytics and accepted a position at Skyfactor in Springfield, Missouri. By the time I started in June 2017 with the Analytics and Research Team (ART), I had already taught myself enough to be fairly compentent in R and SQL, and my director was keen to see me develop those skills further by adding Python to my repertoire. I held that position for two years. During that time I rewrote the desktop application used by ART to run Monte Carlo simulations and build predictive models for student success. I also built a couple of console applications to automate some annoyingly tedious aspects of our production process and wrangle data for our operations and finance team.

In June 2019, I accepted a position at DealCloud in Charlotte, North Carolina, again as a data analyst. I hated the winters in the midwest, and having been raised in the Carolinas I was happy to move back home. I was also excited about the opportunity because, unlike my first job which had at least something to do with my graduate degree in terms of the industry and the subject matter, DealCloud served the financial services industry exclusively. I was hired specifically because of the technical skills I had managed to teach myself up to that point, and I was proud of that. I had two full years of experience with Python under my belt, and even though I preferred R for a long time, that had certainly changed by then.

At DealCloud, my work dealt with migrating new clients' data from their old solution into their instance of our app. After 6 months of working on data migrations, I transitioned into my first official software developer role on the Technical Services team where my focus shifted away from any one specific client's migration to developing solutions that could help with all migrations. I worked on my first full stack application there: a Flask app with a SQLite database and a React frontend. It had been designed and built by another developer who had left the company a few months before I arrived. It used a Celery task queue to process data asynchronously and ran on Ubuntu. I was responsible for designing and developing fixes and enhancements, and because it was a small project I also managed the environments on the server, built it, and deployed it. So in addition to SQL, R, and Python, I added Bash and the frontend dev stack—HTML, CSS, and JavaScript—to my skillset.

In August 2020, I moved into my current role with Signature. Python is still my preferred language, and although I can do both, I enjoy server-side work much more than client-side. I'm love logic, mathematics, philosophy, philology, physics, and computers. I also still have a heart for education, and I hope that one day my career will lead back to that field. During the COVID-19 pandemic, I started playing and and studying chess, which has been strangely therapeutic. I enjoy road cycling, hiking, and backpacking in the Great Smoky Mountains.
